Race Format

The BMAI 8-Hour Relay is a team endurance relay race held on a 400-metre track. The objective is for each team to cover the greatest total distance within eight (8) hours. Each team consists of eight (8) runners. Runners compete in a fixed running order. Each runner completes two (2) relay legs of approximately 30 minutes each, for a total running time of approximately one (1) hour. After all eight runners complete their first relay leg, the team repeats the same running order for the second relay leg. The team with the greatest adjusted total distance at the end of eight (8) hours will be declared the winner of its division.

Divisions

The event consists of three divisions:

  • Open Division
  • Mixed Division
  • Masters Division

Each team must consist of eight (8) registered runners. More details follow:

Open Division

  • All runners must be 15 years of age or older on Race Day.
  • Participants aged 15–17 must have a parent or legal guardian sign the event waiver prior to participating.
  • No gender restrictions.
  • Teams may consist of any combination of male and female runners.

Mixed Division

  • All runners must be 15 years of age or older on Race Day.
  • Participants aged 15–17 must have a parent or legal guardian sign the event waiver prior to participating.
  • Each team must consist of 4 male runners and 4 female runners.

Masters Division

  • All runners must be 50 years of age or older on Race Day.
  • Standard team composition: 4 men and 4 women.

Distance Adjustment:

For every one (1) additional male runner above four (4), 1 kilometre will be deducted from the team's final distance. For every one (1) additional female runner above four (4), 1 kilometre will be added to the team's final distance. i.e 4M/4F = 0 km, 5M/3F = -1 km, 6M/2F = -2 km, 7M/1F = -3 km, 8M/0F = -4 km, 3M/5F = +1 km, 2M/6F = +2 km, 1M/7F = +3 km, 0M/8F = +4 km,

Relay Leg Rules

Each runner must complete exactly two (2) relay legs. The official duration of each relay leg is 30 minutes. Because the race is conducted on a 400-metre track, relay exchanges must take place only after the incoming runner completes a full lap. Each relay leg may vary by up to three (3) minutes (27–33 minutes) to allow exchanges after a full lap. Each team will be provided with one official Relay Tag for timing purposes. The Relay Tag must be transferred between teammates within the designated Exchange Zone. Teams are responsible for ensuring the Relay Tag is transferred correctly. Failure to properly transfer or carry the Relay Tag may result in missed lap records, penalties, or disqualification. Each runner may complete only two relay legs unless otherwise approved by the Race Director under exceptional circumstances.

Team Registration & Running Order

By the registration deadline, each team must submit its team name, team captain, eight registered runners, division, and running order (Runners 1–8). The running order is considered final after registration closes unless approved by the Race Director.

Scoring

The official result is based on the total distance completed within eight (8) hours. Masters Division adjustments will be applied after the race.

Awards

Championship awards will be presented to the winning team in each division: Open Division, Mixed Division, and Masters Division.

Relay Exchange Zone

All relay exchanges must take place within the designated Exchange Zone. Failure to do so may result in a penalty.

Timing and Lap Counting

The official race time is eight (8) hours. Lap counting stops immediately when the official race time expires. Only laps fully completed before the official finish time will be counted.

General Rules

  • Each participant may compete for one team only.
  • All runners must wear their official race bib while competing.
  • Teams must follow all instructions from race officials and volunteers.
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct, cheating, or rule violations may result in penalties or disqualification.

Safety and Conduct

Participants must compete safely and in a sportsmanlike manner. Obstructing or intentionally interfering with another runner is prohibited.

Rule Interpretation and Amendments

The Race Director reserves the right to interpret, amend, and enforce these rules. The Event Organizer may modify these rules prior to Race Day if necessary to ensure safety, fairness, and the smooth operation of the event. Any changes will be communicated to all registered teams before Race Day.
